About Us

Learn who we are, what we stand for, and how our mission to empower learners like you drives everything we do.


Mission

To uplift and empower South African domestic workers, gardeners, and underrepresented individuals by providing affordable, accessible, and practical skills training that improves employability, boosts earnings, and encourages self-employment.


Vision

To uplift and empower South African domestic workers, gardeners, and underrepresented individuals by providing affordable, accessible, and practical skills training that improves employability, boosts earnings, and encourages self-employment.


Goal

To become South Africa’s leading provider of vocational and life skills training for underserved communities by offering mobile-first, easy-to-access courses rooted in practical, real-world impact.

Why This Work Matters

In South Africa, millions of domestic workers and gardeners serve families every day but are often undervalued, underpaid, and left without growth opportunities. Many lack basic education, digital skills, or access to formal training. Empowering the Nation exists to change that.


We believe that by teaching real-life skills—from first aid and sewing to life literacy and cooking—we can shift lives, unlock potential, and transform households from the inside out. Our courses do more than upskill — they empower.

History

Discover the journey that shaped our institution, from humble beginnings to a trusted place for skills and growth.


Empowering the Nation was founded in 2022 as a grassroots initiative to address the growing gap in affordable vocational training. The business started in Soweto with just one sewing machine and a borrowed community hall. Since then, we’ve trained over 250 learners, grown our curriculum to include eight unique programmes, and expanded into mobile training across Johannesburg. Our services now include six-month and six-week course options, offered both in person and through our user-friendly app and website.


Precious Radebe, the founder and heart behind Empowering the Nation, was raised in a working-class family in Johannesburg. Her mother worked as a domestic helper for over 30 years, supporting five children on minimum wage. Precious saw firsthand the struggles and sacrifices involved—and knew there had to be a better way. After earning a diploma in community development and working in youth empowerment projects, she launched Empowering the Nation in honour of her mother and countless others like her. Today, Precious leads a small but dedicated team of instructors and coordinators who share her mission to uplift and educate.
